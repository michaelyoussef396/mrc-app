System Overview

Mould & Restoration Co. — How the Operations System Works

A five-minute, plain-English tour of the platform that runs every MRC job — from a customer's first website enquiry to the final paid invoice. Written for operations, finance, and partners.

1.What MRC Is

Mould & Restoration Co. is a Melbourne mould inspection and remediation business.

The MRC App is the single system that runs every job from the customer's first website enquiry through to the final paid invoice — booking the visit, capturing what the technician finds on-site, sending the inspection report, performing the remediation work, billing the customer, and tracking payment.

It is built mobile-first so technicians can use it on a phone in the field, and it works offline at job sites with no signal — syncing automatically once they're back in coverage.

2.Who Uses It

Three groups interact with the system. Two of them log in; the third never does.

An Admin (office team)

can see every customer and every job in the pipeline. They book inspections, assign technicians, review and approve reports before they leave the building, send invoices, and track who has paid. This is the only role that touches money or sends communication to customers.

A Field Technician

can see only the jobs assigned to them. They fill out the inspection form on-site, take photos, and later fill out a job completion form once remediation work is finished. They cannot view other technicians' jobs, the invoicing area, or the broader admin dashboards.

A Customer

submits the initial enquiry through the public website form. They never log in. Every piece of communication they receive — booking confirmation, inspection report, job report, invoice, review request — arrives by email.

3.The Job Lifecycle

Each customer file moves through eight business stages. The label under each stage shows who is responsible for moving it forward.

4.Core Business Objects

Beneath the surface, the system organises everything around nine business objects. These are the things you'd recognise from a paper-based operation — customers, jobs, photos, invoices — translated into a structured digital record.

Customer

A person or business who has asked for our help. Holds contact details, the property address, who is assigned, and the current pipeline status.

Inspection

The first site visit. Records what the technician found (mould locations, moisture readings, climate data, photos), the recommended treatment, and the price quote.

Area

One room within an inspection (e.g. main bathroom, ensuite, laundry). Each area has its own observations, moisture readings, and photo set.

Booking

A specific appointment in the calendar. Holds the date, time, technician assigned, and the travel time from the previous job.

Job Report

The record of what was actually done during the remediation work. Lists treatment methods, chemicals applied, equipment deployed, and any scope changes from the original quote.

Invoice

The bill sent to the customer. Tracks line items, GST, discount (capped at 13%), payment status (draft, sent, paid, overdue), and payment method.

Photo

A piece of visual evidence. Tagged as before, after, or general, and attached to the relevant area or job report.

Technician

A staff member who does the field work. Has a profile, a calendar of assigned bookings, and a private view of their own jobs.

Activity

A single event in the timeline of a customer's file (e.g. "inspection booked for 22 April", "report approved by admin", "payment received"). Activities make up the audit trail visible on every customer record.

5.How the Parts Connect

The MRC App is a website hosted in the cloud. Everything a user sees and clicks runs in their browser. The database, login, and file storage live on a managed backend service. Four outside services are called only when needed — when an email is sent, when a team notification fires, when travel time needs estimating, and when an inspection summary is drafted by AI.

6.Key Screens

The system is built around about a dozen primary screens. These are the ones an operations manager would want to know exist.

  • Admin DashboardToday's snapshot — jobs scheduled, leads to assign, completed this week, revenue this week, and any reports awaiting review.
  • Lead ListThe pipeline view of every customer, filterable by stage and searchable.
  • Customer DetailThe most-used admin screen — contact, property, inspection findings, quote, booking, job report, invoice, payment status, and the full activity timeline on one page.
  • ScheduleA week-or-day calendar of every technician's bookings, with drag-to-reassign.
  • Reports & AnalyticsConversion rate, average revenue per job, completion time, and breakdown by lead source.
  • Inspection Report EditorWhere admin reviews the draft inspection PDF, edits any field inline, and sends it to the customer.
  • Job Report EditorThe same screen pattern for the post-remediation report.
  • Technician DashboardToday's jobs, with a "next job" card showing address and travel time from the technician's current location.
  • My JobsA technician's full assigned-job list, grouped by today, this week, upcoming, and completed.
  • Inspection FormThe mobile form a technician fills out on-site — multi-section, auto-saves every few seconds, and works offline.
  • Job Completion FormThe mobile form a technician fills out after the remediation work is finished — captures methods, chemicals, equipment used, and before/after photos.
  • Public Request FormThe website page where prospective customers submit their initial enquiry. The only screen accessible without logging in.
